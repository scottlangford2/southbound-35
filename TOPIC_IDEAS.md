# Blog Topic Ideas

Candidate topics for future Southbound 35 posts. Organized by theme and
anchored to the blog's existing focus on public finance and economic
development along the Texas I-35 corridor, with occasional sports and
religion essays.

## Threads already teed up by existing posts

1. **The voided $440M Hays County road bond** — what the Tex. Elec. Code
   §4.003 notice defect actually was, and how often Texas bond elections
   get reversed on procedural grounds.
2. **Hays County's first comprehensive water study since 2011** — Edwards
   Aquifer, rule of capture, and what 612K residents mean for groundwater.
   Natural pair with the projections post.
3. **Kyle's warehouse economy** — is Central Texas logistics a durable tax
   base or a low-wage filler? Property-tax-per-acre of distribution vs.
   retail vs. residential.

## Public finance along the corridor

4. **TIRZs and 380 agreements between New Braunfels and Belton** — who's
   using them and where the incidence falls.
5. **MUDs in Hays and Comal** — governance, debt loads, and what happens
   on annexation.
6. **SH 130 vs. I-35** — the toll road's second life as a freight bypass
   and what that means for I-35 expansion.

## Economic development and demographics

7. **The "Austin doughnut"** — Travis's flat core vs. Hays / Williamson /
   Bastrop / Caldwell rings, tested against the monocentric city model.
8. **Texas State as anchor institution** — San Marcos's town-gown fiscal
   balance sheet; compare to Denton/UNT and College Station/A&M.
9. **Data centers and ERCOT load growth on I-35** — siting, transmission,
   and who pays for grid upgrades.

## Public health and environment

10. **Flash-flood alley economics** — flood-insurance take-up and
    repetitive-loss properties along the Blanco, San Marcos, and
    Guadalupe.
11. **Home insurance rates in Central Texas** — hail, wildfire, and the
    non-renewal wave.

## Sports and religion

12. **Rangers payroll and the small-market myth post-2023** — a
    Moneyball-ish look at Texas baseball economics.
13. **Another *hevel* essay** — e.g., Ruth as a migration story read
    against Hays County in-migrants, or Nehemiah on rebuilding public
    infrastructure.
